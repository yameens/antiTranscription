CS131 Final Project Guidelines
Spring 2026

The final project is your opportunity to apply the computer vision principles learned in class to a problem of your choice. Whether you want to dive deep into a specific algorithm (like 3D reconstruction or motion tracking) or apply CV to a hobby (like sports analytics or botany), the goal is creativity and technical rigor.
Project Logistics
Groups: You may work individually or in groups of up to 3. Expectations for scope and depth increase with group size.
Mentor: Each group will be assigned a TA mentor to provide guidance.
Submission: All components must be submitted via Gradescope.
Deadlines:
Project Proposal Due: Friday 5/15, 11:59 pm
Project Milestone Due: Friday 5/22, 11:59 pm
Final Demo Day Presentation Slides Due: Tuesday 6/2, 11:59 pm
Final Demo Day: Monday 6/1 (virtual), Wednesday 6/3 (in-person) - Location and Time TBA on Ed
Final Project Report Due: Saturday 6/6, 11:59 pm


Component 1: Project Proposal (5%)
Format: 1-page PDF, submitted via Gradescope.
Goal: Define a clear problem, state your proposed methodology, and identify initial steps.
Rubric (5% total):
Problem Statement & Originality (2%): Originality and creativity in the chosen problem with clear and concise descriptions. Is the problem well-defined and creative?
Proposed Methodology (2%): Coherence and clarity in the proposed approach. Appropriateness of the methodology for the problem. Is the technical approach sound and relevant to CS 131?
Feasibility & Timeline (1%): A realistic and achievable timeline with objectives. Can this realistically be completed in ~4 weeks?
Component 2: Project Milestone (5%)
Format: 2-page PDF, submitted via Gradescope.
Goal: Summarize your achievements and any deviations from the original proposal. Showcase an intermediate result through visualization. Provide an updated timeline and objectives.
Rubric (5% total): 
Technical Progress (2%): Substantive evidence of implementation such as data processing, feature extraction, or initial model results. Is the progress technically sound and aligned with the proposal?
Visualization & Results (2%): Clear and insightful visualizations of intermediate outputs. Does the visual evidence demonstrate early data exploration, a functional pipeline or identify specific areas for improvement?
Updated Timeline & Plan (1%): A refined schedule for the remaining tasks including any pivots from the original scope. Is the roadmap to the final submission realistic and well-defined?
Component 3: Demo Day & Final Report (30%)
A. Demo Day Presentation
Format: 4 minute oral presentation with visual aids (e.g., slides, demos). Submit your final demo day presentation slides on Gradescope.
Content: Overview of the project, methodology, results, and learnings.
Rubric:
Clarity of Problem Statement and Proposed Methodology (7%): 
Clarity of Results, Analysis, and Discussion (7%)
Quality of Presentation (6%)
B. Final Report
Format: 4-page PDF using the CVPR LaTeX template.
Structure: 
Introduction: Motivation, significance, and challenges of the chosen problem.
Related Work: Discuss related research and differentiate your approach.
Methodology: Detail your methods, algorithms, and models used.
Results: Present your findings with quantitative and qualitative analysis.
Conclusion: Summarize your findings, their implications, and potential future work.
Individual Contributions: For group projects, clearly state each member's contributions (what code each one wrote, experiments run, etc).
Rubrics:
Introduction and Motivation (5%)
Depth of Research and Related Work (10%)
Methodology and Technical Rigor (10%)
Results, Analysis, and Discussion (20%)
Writing Quality and Organization (5%)



Notes:
You may choose to work individually or in a group (up to 3 members).
No late dates can be applied to the Demo Day Slides and Final Report.
Please have only one group member submit the proposal and select the other group members during submission.
Group members must contribute equally. For teams with 3 people, the project will be graded with higher expectations and should demonstrate clear contributions from all 3 students.
Please email your TA mentor directly for project-specific questions and guidance. For general questions related to computer vision, please use Ed, come to TA office hours, or book office hours with Professors Niebles and Gaidon.
You are free to use any programming language and libraries. Proper citations and acknowledgments are required.
